Welcome to our Regression Analysis Platform

Cereal Systems Initiative for South Asia (CSISA) (led by the International Maize and Wheat Improvement Centre (CIMMYT)) and Extension Division of the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) has deployed Landscape Diagnostic Survey (LDS). The survey captured production practices applied by farmers in Rice and Wheat crops. The survey recorded a large number of variables covering major crop production factors such as previous crop, residue management, crop establishment method, variety and seed sources, nutrient management, irrigation management, weed flora and their management, harvesting method and farmer reported yield.

The LDS analysis can identify mega or large trends across regions (e.g. variety/hybrid use), interactions and more local or ecology specific contexts (e.g. the performance of long vs medium duration varieties in different ecologies), and importantly identify new categories or groupings to target and understand adoption (e.g. by types and number of irrigations). This ecosystem requires an integrated effort on the part of biological and social scientists to diagnose issues faster and find solutions accordingly. To achieve this, it is required to find out the variables/factors, which are majorly contributing towards determining the yield of rice and wheat.

In this Web Application, Machine learning algorithms viz. Stepwise regression, LASSO (least absolute shrinkage and selection operator), Ridge Regression and Random Forest Regression can be used for feature selection. The selected features are employed for crop yield prediction using Multiple Linear Regression (MLR), Support Vector Regression, Ridge Regression and Random Forest Regression. The accuracy of the models are computed using diagnostic measures viz. Coefficient of determination (R2), Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) and Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE).
